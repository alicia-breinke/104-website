The Issue of Misinformation

As already implied, social media has its advantages. At the same time, however, it often raises questions about the legitimacy of the information that it provides. Moreover, this misinformation can have major consequences but there are some companies that have yet to effectively address this issue. “Social Media and Fake News in the 2016 Election,” for instance, suggests that this news can be misleading for peoples’ beliefs, make it harder to select quality candidates, and make it hard for consumers to distinguish inaccurate information from legitimate information. Additionially, Kaya Yurieff’s “How Twitter, Facebook and YouTube are handling election misinformation,” suggests that the three major social media platforms- Twitter, YouTube, and Facebook- used different strategies to curve misleading claims about the election. As stated by Yurieff, “Twitter has gone as far as reducing users' ability to share misleading posts, while Facebook is slapping labels on misinformation, but not hindering sharing. YouTube is taking arguably the least aggressive action, by relying on a single label -- which reminds people that US election results may not be final -- on any and all content related to the election.”
